Every individual participant in a State would like to be adequately rewarded for their efforts to make their State stronger. 

If individuals aren't adequately rewarded, it costs them energy, and they relatively lose status in the process (creating a tragedy of the commons).

The Parenting Function creates a virtuous cycle by incentivizing 
every Individual Actor in a State to always 
be on the lookout for ONLY potentially valuable members, 
and invite them to their Synchronized State,
in order to reap economic 
rewards proportional to the value
these new members create.

The Parenting Function is responsible for adding new Actors into the system. It is a risk Function, mimicking parenting in real life and forms Family Trees.

Society Protocol operates as a permissioned protocol, meaning: Individual Actors need to either be a part of the Genesis Actors or be invited to the instance by an Actor who already has an account and is willing to risk Energy to invite them via the Parenting Function. 

The Parenting Function is a mechanism designed to incentivize the invitation of ONLY individuals perceived as valuable into the society protocol instance. 

Game Theory: Incentives encourage the Parent(s) to risk some amount of Energy to invite an entity they perceive as being valuable, with the intention to recoup a surplus of Energy over time––as that Child grows and earns Energy within the system–– while declining to invite entities who they perceive aren't worth the energetic costs. ^c5RkA8GU

Parents can choose to allocate (risk) Energy towards having Children. The invite creates a Birth Event during which a new Actor is created in the protocol and tethered to the Parent account. This tether forms an explicit Family Tree structure. Afterwards, whenever the Child earns Energy, a portion of it trickles up the Family Tree to their Parent(s). ^vBTcbRqi
