The Hunting Value Function is responsible for redistributing value across the network. It is a staking Function, which mimics hunting in real life.

Hunters stake their Energy for a chance to win the Hunt, which happens at each Timeslot in the protocol, redistributing Energy. In return, they must provide the system with Data Availability (the ability to "see" Events).

To be able to Hunt, Hunters must be able to "see", and that generally means seeing Events unfolding in the system (which translates to inputting transactions). This incentivizes the Hunters to add all Events occurring in the system to the network, (potentially) without requiring any sort of fees to be paid on Events themselves. 

Hunting can be perceived as the inverse of staking in modern blockchain protocols. Rather than a staker getting inflationary rewards, (which dilute all other participants in the protocol), hunters receive a bigger share of the zero-sum Energy pie for their services from others in the protocol. The value lost by other Actors in the probabilistic lottery is exactly proportional to their value to the protocol via the Activity Score. 

This lottery stochastically redistributes Energy over time in the protocol. ^S14Km65N

Activity Score – Each Actor's account in Society Protocol has an Activity Score attached to it which measures how "alive" they have been in the network. 

Hunter – Each Timeslot, one or multiple Hunters are selected to input Events into the protocol, and be rewarded with the Energy of the Hunted parties.

Hunted – Each Timeslot, one or multiple parties are chosen to be Hunted in the protocol in a probabilistic lottery in which their chances to win are based off their Activity Score.

Hunt – Each Timeslot, there is a Hunt in which the Energy of the Hunted parties gets redistributed to the Hunter(s) ^ZZGBHPga

During each Timeslot in Society Protocol, 
a Hunt occurs. This Hunt is a probabilistic lottery which
one or more Actors win––entitling them to be Hunted (it's not a lottery you that they want to win).

During the Hunt, Energy is transferred from the Hunted Actors to the Hunter Actors.

The chances of winning this probabilistic lottery for each Actor is inverse of their Activity Score (meaning Actors with higher Activity Scores are less likely to be the Hunted party at each Timeslot).

Actors can participate to win this lottery by staking their Energy in the Hunting Function. In most variations of Society Protocol, the Hunting Function is responsible for inputting Events into the system. 

Hunters are more likely to win the Hunt at each Timeslot based on how many Events they "see" and input into the network. The more Events they input, the greater their chance to be the Hunter or greater Hunt for that Timeslot. 

Thus, Hunters provide Data Availability (DA) to the network. ^marWDBno
