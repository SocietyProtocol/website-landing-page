The Curation Value Function helps subjectively sorting Actors, Organizations, and Property in Society Protocol. It mimics as gossip in real life. 

Curators risk Energy to subjectively and objectively rate Actors, Organizations, and Property in the network, with the ability to recoup more Energy (as a reward for content discoverability).

Curation isn't purely positive or negative (objective), it's meaning lies in exposing the true characteristics of the Curated (subjective). We are looking to surface valuable information about Actors, Property, and Organizations using economic incentives. 

Curation encompasses multi-dimensional ratings (subjective), objective ratings ranges, and multiple Curation Containers (to prevent one hierarchy and mass 1st likes). This allows participants to show valuations of Actors, Property, and Organizations in different subjective prescriptions, objective ratings, and hierarchies.

Leaving a subjective comment (container) can add more data to the reviewed object, and Curators are incentivized to add quality descriptions because they want their Curation Container to succeed.

The most generic and widely held beliefs in society should surface as a result and become the most successful Curation Containers...Surfacing the most accurate and widely held beliefs into the shared reality for everyones benefit. 

Every niche type of more detailed Curation Container should also proliferate, based on exactly the reflection of how many participants believe others share this niche belief. 

Game Theory: Individual Actors are incentivized to pay the Unit Price to participate in a Curation Container––if they believe they are "early". Meaning, if they believe that they are ahead of the profitable median. It offers diminishing returns on attaching Curation Slots to the Curation Container, until the market reaches equilibrium (when people aren't willing to add to the Curation Container).

Curation aligns incentives in such a way that incentivizes Actors to reveal valuable information (which they believe a significant subset of other Curators would agree with) to the wider network, in exchange for economic benefits proportional their value in discovering this information (they earlier they participate to reveal accurate shared perceptions, the more upside).

Based on the objective rating range: if the object is deemed valuable to society...then the Actor, Property, or Organization itself can be rewarded. The Curation mechanism directly rewards both: objects which are undervalued in the eyes of the network and the Curators (who provide discoverability for them). 

In the end, society receives accurate subjective and objective ratings, benefitting society, the objects being curated, and the curators at the expense of unsuccessful curators, who do not bring any discoverability value. ^l7VzmLhI

Any Actor can place a Curation Container on any Actor, Property Object, or Organization.

A Curation Container costs Energy to create, which trickles down to the object being curated, or dissipates to other entities (potentially disperses to all Actors) depending on the objective: positive or negative signal (this signal can be a ranged value).

A Curation Container is created with three pieces of information:
1) A subjective opinion about the curated item. (Example: This house is clean and spacious.)
2) A Perspective (a limiting set of characteristics of Actors and Organizations who can participate in this Curation Container). 
3) A Unit Price (the Energy cost to mint a Curation Slot in this Curation Container)

After a Curation Container is created, anyone that fits within its Perspective can participate in it, signaling their agreement with the subjective view of the Curator by paying the Unit Price. 

Paying the Unit Price and participating in the Curation Container 
adds that Actor in a linear sequential order to those Actors who have chosen to participate in that Curation Container and paid the Unit Price before them. The Unit Price paid trickles down the Curation Container, paying dividends to every Actor that came before them and potentially trickling down to the original object (depending on the objective: positive or negative signal).  ^5zUlyfVc

Curator – The Actor or Organization who is doing the curating. I.E. either creating a Curation Container or minting a Curation Slot. 
Curated – The Actor, Property, or Organization which is being analyzed.
Curation Container – A container filled with subjective information which is attached to the Curated and forms the root for a chain of Curation Slots. 
Perspective – The limitation list of the Actors and Organizations who are allowed to participate in this Curation Container. 
Curation Unit Price (CUP) – The price to participate in a Curation Container and mint a single Curation Slot.
Curation Slot – An NFT received for participating in a Curation Container which collects royalties from other Curation Slots which are minted afterwards. ^R2jh7UHL
