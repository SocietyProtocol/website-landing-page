Energy Reserve ^sb675NIW

Actors can pay Energy to create, 
read, update, and delete (CRUD) programmable 
objects which are the Property of Actors 
or Organizations within the system. 

Property includes currencies, creative content, 
decentralized exchanges,
title deeds, NFTs, and any other
turing complete programmable
property. 
 ^ZmdjcW94

Actors can stake Energy to Hunting.
At each Time Interval, the system
redistributes an amount of Energy
to the Hunters from the randomly 
chosen Hunted parties in the 
system. This action is a lottery 
performed stochastically based 
on the Activity Scores of all 
Actors in the system. To put it
simply: the higher Activity 
Score, the less chance an 
Actor has to be Hunted. ^F4DwXsNV

Actors can risk Energy to parent,
creating new Actors in the Society
Protocol instance. 

Parenting risks Energy, which can be 
recouped by the Parent via the Family
Tree bonds. After new Actors are 
created in the system, an amount  
of all the Energy they earn is redistributed
to the Parent Actor. ^A09hgdZv

Actors can pay Energy,
to create and manage Organizations.

Society Protocol has two 
distinct types of Organization: Distributed Corporations (dCorps) and Cultural Agreements (Cultures).
 ^CPGh5A7z

Distributed Corporations are programmable 
DAOs and Cultural Agreements are rulesets
for participants in the Culture. ^JWsPRaT3

Actors can pay Energy,
to access verifiable 
programmable Communication 
with others in the system. ^n74QCzII

These types of Communication 
can be private, semi-private, or public
(encrypted by the participants
E2EE and hosted by the Farmers).

Programmable Communication includes
group chats, forums, email,
and announcements. ^GVykoEBl

Actors can stake Energy to Farm.
Farming (data) rewards
Actors for storing the data generated 
in a Society Protocol instance securely
and in a distributed verifiable manner. ^QHSyJIvI

Actors can risk Energy to Curate,
attaching a Curation Container on 
any Actor, Property, or Organization.

Curation Containers are labelled by
subjective belief, (examples: this Property is 
durable, this Actor is trustworthy).

 ^83Vb7KVZ

Curation Containers cost Energy 
to create, but can recoup 
much more if other Curators
share beliefs,
by forming a 
pyramid. 
 ^wEm3PASE

Actors can stake Energy to Portal,
Portal functionality allows groups of 
Actors to exit a Society Protocol instance as verifiable
identities and enter into a new instance (fork)
with different Parameters. Actors' Energy in the 
old instance will dissipate to the participants who remain.  ^Ribp48mg

Actors can risk Energy
to govern the protocol.
The Governance Function is
separated into two parts: ^H1YUhs03

Reserve

unallocated 
Energy. ^MhFEc7zt

2. [[Function-Governance (Futarchy)|Futarchy]] (Seers): 
In Futarchy, Seers compete to make the 
most accurate decisions about potential 
policy using prediction markets. ^g2t1UK8v
