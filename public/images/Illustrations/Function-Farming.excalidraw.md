Society needs its state stored somewhere, yet the act of storing it with centralized intermediaries in data centers creates profound imbalances in power structures at the social layer.

Farmers compete globally to provide society with state storage. This distributed competition increases redundancy, fault tolerance, removes centralization, and provides society with the best economic rates available for the storage of it's state. 

Farmers give society a more sophisticated technology to store it's state data, removing the need for centralized control of the state
and the centralization of power which 
arises from that. 

 ^6Xnzwr5r

Just like agriculture invented a more sophisticated way for Hunters to earn a greater yield of caloric energy, and much of civilization turned to agriculture from hunting and gathering tribes: Farming is the same way.

Farming offers a little bit more sophisticated and resource intensive way, which requires machinery, for Actors and Organizations to earn a yield of Energy in exchange for their contribution of watering the data crops of Society Protocol).

 ^Q5gfL1fh

Farmers are state providers who store and maintain the state. They are responsible for storing the state of Society Protocol.

It mimics farming in real life. Instead of farming crops, Society Protocol Farmers grow the state data. They water the data crops and keep the planet well fed with information and (hopefully) green. 

Farmers store the data in a distributed fashion, allowing everyone else (outside of Farmers and Hunters) to use the lightest clients to access their accounts (as they aren't required to run nodes) allowing for mass mobile access to the protocol. ^XG0sROI7

Farmers can enshrine the current state hash of the protocol at each Timeslot in the Timeline. 

It is likely possible to require Farmers to store the public state of Society Protocol for free (curation, governance, state hash, etc), 
in exchange for the opportunity to earn Energy from payment fees of the Property, Organization, and Communication Value Functions. ^JMZZUS6U

When new state is generated in society, it's often desirable to have it stored somewhere securely. 

The Farmers stake Energy for the opportunity and responsibility to store this archival and live data.  (including public, encrypted semi-public, and private) state. 

Actors and Organizations in society pay the Farmers Energy for this service when they use the Property, Organizations, and Communication Functions.

This data becomes distributed, stored and maintained by the Farmers (with an adequate redundancy factor to protect the state from being lost or corrupted). If the Farmers don't adequately retain this state, their Energy stake becomes slashed and redistributed.  

Farmers store 3 types of state data: 
1. long-term storage blobs (i.e: e2e encrypted communications, dmails)
2. quickly accessible and changing data (dapps, public state)
3. indexes (a grouping of certain types of data)

Over time, the Farming mechanic will grow more complex and optimized to optimize for these functions, but the basis is simple.  ^RTB2nT3C
