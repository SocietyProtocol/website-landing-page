Actors pay Energy to communicate verifiably with other Actors in the system. This Energy generally goes to the Farmers for storage and access to these communications. 

It is a lot cheaper per byte than interactions with Property or Organizations because Communication data is mostly hosted and stored as blobs encrypted off-chain by the Farmers. This state is not interactive, nor always accessible publicly. It's large quantities of data that the Farmers store off-chain for various Actors to be able to access to communicate with each other in a verifiable and programmable way.

Communication interactions are intertwined with all the other artifacts produced in the system. Individual Actors can set very specific rules around how they want to Communicate. For example:

    1. Unknowns can only message me if they send me 3.5 Energy, and only if we share at least two first-order mutual connection.
    2. Actors which haven't existed in the system for at least 3 years cannot contact me, without first presenting a referral from someone that has. 
    3. Only those in my Family Tree can contact me.
  
All of this programmability can interact with identities, Property, Organizations, Energy, etc...as a wrapper around the actual Communications in the system. 

While Communication normally goes through the Farmers for servers and storage (and don't necessarily have to be in the Timeline or Events in the system).

It doesn't necessarily have to run through the Farmers. Alternatively, anyone can host their own public or private servers, which store Communications (encrypted or public) deriving from Actors accounts in Society Protocol. ^vJ8ehQs9

Communication enables coordination, and better coordinated societies survive and conquer ones with inferior coordination.

Society Protocol enables direct, distributed, verifiable, and programmable communication between Actors––bearing the entire weight of their identities and economic incentives, without any man in the middle (MITM).  ^nn8vLPQs

The Communication Function allows the users of Society Protocol to have public discourse (public state), private communications (semi-private state), and even a verifiable inner world (private state) in a truly correctly tiered fashion of access.

The system automatically provides storage for these communications in the form of the Farmers, who store data produced in the Communication Function at the right level of encryption, in exchange for payments of Energy. Alternatively, the data itself can be theoretically stored and hosted anywhere, using off-chain clients and servers connected to the users private-public keypair of accounts in Society Protocol (it's not required to use the Farming Function).  

The true power in the Communication Function of Society Protocol lies in it's identity, programmability, verifiability, distribution, and extensibility.

    1. Identity – Every Communication Function interaction is tied to the full weight of each Actors identity and history. Users are always able to see the entire public history of their counterparties interactions: who they are connected to, what state they have introduced into the system, how much Energy they have, how they earned it, etc...

    2. Programmability –  Account holders get complete control over whom, what, where, and how can Communicate with them. Users are able to set algorithmic automatically enforced rules down to the micro-details––gathering from all the identity data in the protocol, and including economics.  

    3. Verifiability – Every Communication Function interaction is verifiable, meaning it can be attributed to the full weight of Actors identities at any point in the future (both publicly and privately). This state is innately verifiably tied to the rest of Actors identities, and even while it may be encrypted and privately held, it remains potentially verifiably attributable in the future. 

    4. Distribution – There is no central source holding everyones Communication Function data and meta-data which everyone must trust to encrypt it correctly and store it safely. The state data is encrypted at the client level of each interacting Actor, and does not need to be stored with a single centralized custodian. 

    5. Extensibility – Every internal and external application, such as ride sharing, a language learning tool, food delivery––can hook into the full public identity of every Individual Actor across every Society Protocol instance by using the Communication Function to facilitate interactions. This works without the application even necessarily needing to exist in Society Protocol.  ^RnNLgyxM
