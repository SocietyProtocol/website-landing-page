Individuals aren't all islands in society, they form into sub-groups. Three ways this is done: is by family (Family Trees), culturally (Cultural Agreements), and economically (Distributed Corporations).

The Organizations Function covers two distinct types of organization structures. 

1) Distributed Corporations (dCorps) – These are an amalgamation of Actors coming together to construct a golem structure. dCorps have Energy, hold Property, have a governance structure to make decisions, and can generally do anything an Individual Actor can do (naturally amplified by the force of the amount of stakeholders in the golem). They generally function exactly as Individual Actors do, except with governance structures for group decision making (which are modular, different for each dCorp) instead of individual accounts. They are meant to coordinate resources better amongst a sub-group with shared intentions.

Instantaneous decision-making globally and programmability can supercharge dCorps compared to traditional corporations from the double-entry period (where decision-making isn't synchronized, history isn't tracked, and everything needs to be a separate duplicated contract enforceable in courts). 

2. Cultural Agreements (Cultures) – Are meant to coordinate individuals, rather than resources. Cultures don't hold Energy, or Property, and don't act anything like an Individual Actor account. Instead, Cultures are rulesets (with both objectively programmable and subjectively interpretable sections) which anyone can create and Actors (or dCorps) can attach to their accounts, binding a group of individuals to a shared way of doing things. 

Unlike our cultures and religions of today (which function on reputation (subjective agreements observed by other members) – Cultural Agreements enforce the coordination of the group automatically via objective rules enforced in the codebase alongside a subjective charter.   ^aHGdf0Dv

You join a Culture which binds your account to a ruleset: "you can't leave this Culture until Death Event". The controlling Actors of the Culture then update the ruleset to say, every Culture member must donate one piece of their Property to the owners each month. You would like to publicly warn others that you're trapped in a cult, but the Culture has a ruleset banning your account from using these words in the public state. ^M7DJ6wX2

You and your friends start an ice cream shop and sell in exchange for Energy. The Energy gets automatically distributed between your ownership shares in the dCorp and paid out to your personal accounts in the right proportion with 25% of the profits held for store improvements. All your expenses are automated with streaming Energy to the vendors, which are likewise dCorps in Society Protocol.  ^9XiGxLzx

You create a new educational institute as a dCorp. It quickly spreads to become profitable, and your group creates a specific Cultural Agreement required for students who want to join and become students. The alumni grow to create many franchises of this institute all over the world, which all function as subsidiary dCorps partially owned by the original dCorp structure you created. ^Ob1LsFUL

Cultural Agreements (Cultures) ^zQLNEto3

Distributed Corporations (dCorps) – Function very much like corporate structures in the real world. dCorps are structural entities, they amalgamate and coordinate multiple Individual Actors into one Organization (structural golem) with its own "life-force" in Energy, which is meant to accomplish the will of a group of Actors. dCorps can do anything Individual Actors in the system can do, but are owned, controlled, and participated in by a group decision making structure involving multiple Individual Actors. They're legal golems.

Distributed Corporations can be pay Energy to function as an Individual Actor themselves after creation, but is steered by a governance structure (which is modularly chosen from a list of variants available in the codebase). 

dCorps can die, just like Individual Actors, when they reach 0 Energy. It's the responsibility of the controlling entities to keep the dCorp alive.

In this sense, dCorp and Individual Actors accounts are very similar account abstractions, with the only difference being that the dCorp is not Parented and controlled by a (multi-sig).   ^NYC0vL7Z

Unlike dCorps, Cultures aren't amalgamations of people per se, rather they are a ruleset. Cultures don't hold Property, conduct business, or channel any Energy together. The only central point in Cultures is a ruleset with a governance structure controlling it. Individual Actors can join a Culture by adding this ruleset to their accounts (binding them to that ruleset objectively and subjectively becoming a part of that sub-group publicly). This ruleset is enforced both objectively (in the codebase) and potentially subjectively (by a governance) layer of the Culture. 

While they don't coordinate resources, Cultures can maintain a governance structure for (which pertains to upgrading the culture, making new rules, accepting new members, and otherwise changing the Culture, etc...) or be headless (immutable and static).

Cultural Agreements can potentially be broken down into: guidelines (rules) and practices (responsibilities) for more distinction. ^3oDaonCg
