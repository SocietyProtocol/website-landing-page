Often––what we say, believe, and pursuade ourselves and others to adopt as policy––doesn't turn out to be what it appears to be, nor the best decision. We are exceedingly good at deceiving both ourselves and others and fighting in persuasive ways for our irrational ideas.

Futarchy is a system which builds economic incentives into accurate decision-making. It is the individualist part of the risk Governance Value Function, which mimics speculation. 

We must all make predictions as individuals in life about uncertain outcomes in the future. Futarchy aims to incentivize those who possess informational advantages to reveal valuable information in our group decision-making (governance) processes, by giving them a prediction market to participate in––where they are compensated for their informational advantage by the market.

All that Futarchy fundamentally does, is give the Governors an opportunity to offload decision making from themselves onto the Seers for any governance decision they encounter in any Governance Domain. It's in this sense a variant of decision making––rather than a governance system. 

Futarchy is a variant of decision making which is broken down into three steps:

    1. A metric to optimize for, and options must be chosen. In Society Protocol this is done by the Governors who already control their respective Governance Domains. Rather than voting or deciding on an option themselves, the Governors can decide it would be easier and/or more efficient to offload this decision to the Seers.
    2. A prediction market is established for these options (with only Seer participants) and the option which the prediction market believes has the highest odds of success towards improving the chosen metric––is implemented as official policy (law, court decision, constitutional change, dispute, etc) of the Society Protocol instance.
    3. After an option is chosen by the Seers, the prediction market remains in-effect and resolves only after measuring the results of the actual implemented policy towards achieving the chosen metric. At which point, the Seers which predicted the results relatively correctly gain Energy at the expense of the incorrect Seers.     

Futarchy is only capable of making group decisions with observable, measurable metrics. It is a good fit for Society Protocol, because the entire protocol is programmable. Essentially, all Governance proposals can be tied to specific programmable objects, and prediction markets can seamlessly be integrated into the Timeline. Society Protocol automatedly keeps track of these metrics in the Timeline––enabling it to establish and resolve these complex prediction markets easily.

Futarchy counterbalances the Distributed Courts side of the Governance Function (where Jurors are incentivized to make decisions which align with the crowd). The biggest economic incentives in Futarchy come from the individual going against the crowd and being right in their prediction.

Individual Actors and Organizations can allocate their Energy, in Futarchy Participation Cost Units (FPCUs), from their Reserve Energy into the Futarchy Function––forming a pool of participating Seers. ^uacGcRac

Whenever any Governance System which controls any of the 5 Governance Domains in Society Protocol is making a decision––they can choose to offload that decision to the Seers via Futarchy––instead of deciding themselves. Either the entire Futarchy Pool  or a randomized subset of Seers is selected to participate in this decision. ^AmJBZn2w

The Governors controlling the Governance System establish a metric to optimize for (such as total Accounts or the amount of Parents in the system) and creates a list of resolution options they're willing to take to maximize this metric. ^yK7oRrIf

The Seers participating in Futarchy are all forced to place bets (using their FPCUs) in every market of options presented to them––estimating their prediction of the impact each option will have on the chosen metric. ^eiUsdK90

The choice which the Seers predict has the highest odds of improving the metric requested by the Governors––is implemented as policy (in the codebase and subjectively) by the protocol instance. ^dIbDzVhB

The Seers FPCUs remains locked in the prediction market, which resolves at a later point in time (this must be specified in the original metric) and pays out each Seer Energy based on the accuracy of their predictions. ^adOpTjrQ
